Let's talk business.


Tell me about yourself.

I moved to Austin in June of 2017 from Wisconsin after graduating college to kickstart my career. Within my coursework, I studied web development, object-oriented programming, networking and computer support. I have two years of applied experience supporting and maintaining computer hardware and software in a help desk setting which I gained from electing to work in the IT Work Study program at my college. In addition to doing work study, I also worked as an adjunct community education instructor, teaching technology literacy classes for a year and a half.


To start my development career, I began working at VersaSuite in the summer of 2017. My responsibilities at VersaSuite included developing, maintaining and optimizing user interfaces in the VersaSuite HIS platform. I had also been appointed as team lead on a handful of projects focusing on optimization / QA of client-facing forms used for the documentation of hospital records.


Upon leaving VersaSuite, I began working for TELUS International as a Geospatial Applications Support Engineer. My responsibilities include providing developer support to users of the Google Maps APIs. This process includes troubleshooting developer-submitted issues and requests while optimizing code and reaching out to developers through platforms such as Stack Overflow and the Google Public Issue Tracker.


What motivates you?

I am highly motivated when I am able to see the efforts of my work in a completed project. I feel a strong sense of pride when I build a working prototype of a website or program and imagine how the end result will be received once it is ready to go live. Looking back and seeing the project I worked on in action is what gives me the inspiration to continue working towards bigger, more meaningful projects.


Where do you see yourself in five years?

Within the next five years, I plan to continue working to learn new technologies and advancing my skills as a web developer. I plan to transition into a more development-oriented role as I work toward my career goal of becoming a front-end engineer.


The fun stuff.


What do you like to do outside of work?

Outside of work, I enjoy mountain biking, listening to music, camping and traveling. I consider myself both an avid car and motorcycle enthusiast with a particular interest in the vintage Japanese variety of both. I have always had an interest in computers and enjoyed playing video games and editing videos as a kid, but I did not begin to develop an interest in pursuing an information technology career until later in high school. As well as enjoying music as a fan, I also play piano and bass guitar. I have played in a handful of bands as both a keyboardist and a bassist.
